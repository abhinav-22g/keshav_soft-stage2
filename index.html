Audiophile Basics

Simple explanations of key concepts

Synergy

Synergy is when the different components of an audio system work together as a single, unified system. It's not about having the most expensive parts, but about the right combination of them. When you pair an amplifier with speakers that complement each other's characteristics, you get a system that sounds better than the sum of its parts.

Impedance

Impedance is the electrical resistance of a speaker to the signal coming from an amplifier. It's measured in Ohms (Ω). A lower impedance speaker (like 4Ω) requires more power from the amp, while a higher impedance speaker (like 8Ω) is more efficient and requires less power.

Sensitivity

Sensitivity tells you how loud a speaker is for a given amount of power. It's measured in decibels (dB) at a distance of one meter with a one-watt input signal. A high-sensitivity speaker (e.g., 90 dB) will play louder than a low-sensitivity speaker with the same power.

Lossless vs. Lossy

Lossless compression (e.g., FLAC, WAV) reduces the file size without removing any of the original audio data. Lossy compression (e.g., MP3, AAC) permanently removes some audio data to make the file much smaller. This allows for easier streaming and storage, but it sacrifices some of the original sound quality.
